Play 01 · The Rarest
Ghost Demand Seeding
The Red Bull Move
Before Red Bull launched, they paid students to go to bars and ask "Do you have Red Bull?" — bartenders started stocking it thinking there was demand. Do this for Tradeclover.

Create 5–10 authentic LinkedIn/WhatsApp/trade forum personas. Have them post: "Has anyone used Tradeclover yet? I heard it's launching soon, trying to decide if I should wait or stay with IndiaMart." Buyers see organic demand. Sellers see buyers asking. You create a self-fulfilling prophecy of interest.

Play 02 · Competitor Jacking
The Complaint Sniper
Set up Google Alerts + Twitter/X searches for: "IndiaMart problem", "TradeIndia fraud", "Alibaba scam supplier", "B2B marketplace cheated". Every single complaint that surfaces — DM that person within 24 hours. Not with a pitch. With a question: "What would an ideal platform do differently?"

You're building a product brief AND a launch customer list simultaneously. These are the HIGHEST-intent leads you'll ever find — people who already tried, already failed, and are actively looking.

Play 06 · Content Judo
The Problem Documentary
Never Mention Your Product
Create a 5-episode YouTube/LinkedIn mini-series: "The Hidden Problems Killing Indian B2B Trade." Interview real traders, exporters, sourcing managers. Episode 1–4: pure pain. Real stories. No solution. Episode 5: "We found one company trying to fix all of this..."

Gary Vaynerchuk calls this "Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook." By episode 5, you have an audience of 10,000+ people who HAVE the exact problem you solve — and they trust you because you showed you understood them before you sold anything.

Play 08 · Association Hack
Trade Association Insider
Identify 20–30 trade associations (FICCI, CII, EEPC, GJEPC, textile guilds, spice boards, pharma exporters). Don't pitch them your platform. Instead — solve THEIR problem for free. Build them a member directory, an event calendar, a news aggregator. Cost: 2 days of dev work each.

In exchange: they feature Tradeclover in their monthly newsletter to their entire membership. One association newsletter = 5,000 pre-qualified buyers/sellers. 20 associations = 100,000 targeted impressions from a trusted source. You didn't buy ads. You earned distribution.

Play 09 · Personal Brand Army
LinkedIn Thought Leader
5 Accounts, 90 Days, Launch Day Bomb
Build 3–5 LinkedIn personal brand accounts for founders/team. For 90 days, post daily about B2B trading pain points — industry data, case studies, how things work. Never mention Tradeclover. Each account grows to 3,000–5,000 followers organically.

Launch day: all 5 accounts post simultaneously. You reach 15,000–25,000 highly targeted industry professionals in a single moment — with zero ad spend. This is how Notion, Linear, and every viral B2B SaaS launches: their team built audiences before anyone knew they were building a product.

Competitor Keyword Ambush
The Cheapest Acquisition Possible
Run Google Search Ads on competitor brand names right now, before you even launch. The ad: "Frustrated with [Competitor]? Something better is coming. Join the Tradeclover waitlist."

People searching a competitor's brand are ALREADY convinced they need a marketplace — they just haven't committed. You intercept them at the highest-intent moment, before they sign up. CPC on brand terms is ₹5–40 vs. ₹500+ for generic category keywords. You're spending 10× less to reach people who are 3× more likely to convert. This is arbitrage, not advertising.
